Hello there, traveller.

What are you doing here?
You must be lost.
That's quite alright.
Everyone who comes here usually is, even if they believe they aren't.

Oh, you want to know where you are?

This is the Front Desk for the Sarajevan Department of Lost Correspondence.
A place for all the things they lost during the siege.

My name?

Around here, they simply call me the Keeper.

I catalogue the things people abandoned.
The things they, perhaps, lost on purpose.
The things they now pretend never existed.

Letters.
Photographs.
Diary entries.
Pages torn from books.
Unsent confessions.
Fragments of lives that were never meant to survive.

Are you looking for a letter?
Although, most are covered in annotations, or handwritten, making them quite hard to read, you can find them in the archive, it's the next door down.

Or maybe a photograph?
I have many of those as well.
They're in the gallery, across from the archive.

...Or perhaps you're looking for someone?

Someone you've lost, and are perhaps hoping to find again.
You might have some luck in the registry, a little bit further down, on your right.

But before you go any further, dear traveller,
I should warn you.

Most of what arrives here was never meant to be found.

Not read.
Not seen.
Not remembered.

These things were meant to disappear quietly beneath everything else.
Buried under noise.
Buried under history.
Buried under people choosing to forget.

Or at least that's what they want to happen.

So be careful while you're here.
You may find something you wish you hadn't.
Something uncomfortable.
Something a bit too human.

But if you do,
don't say I didn't warn you.

Good luck, traveller.

And welcome to the Sarajevan Department of Lost Correspondence.
