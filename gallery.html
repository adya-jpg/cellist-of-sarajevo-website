Stories.

"I remember the day I came home before the Siege officially started.

My mother was calling me in for dinner.

I remember pouting and saying goodbye to my friends as I ran toward the house.

She was cooking soup, and I remember its smell filling the house, mingling with the scent of lilies. She loved lilies.

I remember groaning and complaining that we'd just had soup yesterday.

I remember her putting a hand on her hip, smiling as she scolded me, saying that one day, when I left for university, I would be begging for this soup.

I smiled, and before I could reply, a wall of fire separated us.

I was thrown backward by the blast, and I remember looking up and seeing her frail body hurled through the air.

I wish I had cried out or run to her.

But I was frozen in place, and I just watched her fall to the ground.

Dead.

I guess that's why what I remember most is the sound of her voice calling my name.

And sometimes I hear it again.

I don't know if it's my mind playing tricks on me, or if it's really her.

But now, instead of calling me to dinner,

she's calling me to death."

— Unknown
